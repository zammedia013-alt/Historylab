📜 Passage Practice

AP Classroom-style primary source analysis — all 9 units

📋 Passage SAQ Rubric (3 pts)✕
1
Part A — Use specific evidence from the document. Identify author perspective/purpose if asked.
1
Part B — Connect source to broader historical development using doc + outside knowledge.
1
Part C — Explain continuity, change, or cause/effect using outside knowledge beyond the doc.
💡 Always quote or paraphrase the document. Name the author. Connect to what you know.

✏️ SAQ Practice

Short Answer — 3 parts, no thesis required, 3–5 sentences each

📌 SAQ Formula: Claim → Specific Evidence → Significance/Explanation. Each part max 5 sentences.
📋 SAQ Rubric (3 pts)✕
1
Part A — Historically defensible claim + specific evidence + explain significance.
1
Part B — Same criteria, different aspect. Claim + evidence + significance.
1
Part C — Often asks continuity/change/similarity/difference. Use outside knowledge.
💡 No thesis needed. 3–5 sentences per part. Name specific people, places, events.

📝 LEQ / FRQ Practice

Long Essay Question — thesis, contextualization, evidence, reasoning, complexity

📌 LEQ Structure: Contextualization → Defensible Thesis → 2+ Specific Evidence Examples → Historical Reasoning (CCOT, Causation, or Comparison) → Complexity Point
📋 LEQ Rubric (6 pts)✕
1
Thesis — Defensible, historically specific claim. Goes beyond restating the prompt.
1
Contextualization — Broader context described AND connected to argument. Min 3–5 sentences.
2
Evidence — 1pt: describe 2+ specific examples. 2pt: use evidence to support argument.
1
Historical Reasoning — Causation, comparison, or CCOT. Must be explicit, not implied.
1
Complexity — Nuance, counter-argument, multiple causes, or cross-period connection.
💡 Structure: Contextualization → Thesis → Evidence body paragraphs → Complexity point.

📚 DBQ Practice

Use ≥6 of 7 docs, source 3+ with HAPP, include outside evidence

📌 DBQ Checklist: ✓ Defensible thesis ✓ Contextualization ✓ Use ≥6 docs ✓ HAPP sourcing on ≥3 docs ✓ Outside evidence ✓ Reasoning ✓ Complexity
📋 DBQ Rubric (7 pts)✕
1
Thesis — Defensible, specific claim. Intro or conclusion.
1
Contextualization — Full paragraph of broader context, linked to argument.
2
Doc Content — 1pt: use 3+ docs. 2pt: use 6+ docs to support argument.
1
HAPP Sourcing — Explain Historical situation, Audience, Purpose, OR POV for 3+ docs.
1
Outside Evidence — At least 1 piece of evidence NOT in the documents.
1
Complexity — Nuanced argument, multiple causes, or cross-period connection.
💡 HAPP tip: explain WHY the source context matters to your argument, don't just identify it.
